\section*{Other symbols}
%%%
The following symbols are used for the illustration of linguistic changes.
%%%
\begin{tabbing}
\TABh \= \kill
\textasciitilde \> variant\\
<  \> \isi{borrowing}\\
$\leftarrow$  \> \isi{derivation} or other synchronic process\\
$\Leftarrow$  \> \isi{grammaticalization}\footnotemark or other diachronic process\\
\end{tabbing}

\footnotetext{Note that the term \emph{grammaticalization} is used for different types of linguistic changes leading to re-analysis of a given construction's grammatical meaning. A prototypical instance in this rather broad sense of grammaticalization is the morphologization of a formerly lexical morpheme to a grammatical morpheme, as the development of definite markers from anaphoric pronouns in \ili{Germanic languages}, e.g.~\ili{English} \textit{the (house)} (\textit{the} $\Leftarrow$ \ili{Old English} \textit{þæt}) and \ili{Swedish} \textit{(hus)-et} (\textit{-et} $\Leftarrow$ \ili{Old Norse} \textit{hið}).}
